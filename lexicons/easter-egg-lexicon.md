# Easter Egg Lexicon

Use this lexicon to control approved easter eggs, reference patterns, activation rules, and optional stylistic phrasing for standard easter egg mode.

## Purpose

This lexicon defines approved easter egg sources, phrasing patterns, and activation behavior so humor and references remain intentional, recognizable, and professionally restrained.

Use this lexicon to:

- supply approved easter eggs when easter eggs are explicitly enabled
- prefer controlled references over improvised ones
- keep humor compatible with clarity, tone, and scope safety

Do not use this lexicon to:

- inject humor when easter eggs are not enabled
- replace direct meaning with references
- introduce obscure or exclusionary jokes

## Enablement Rules

Do not use easter eggs by default.

Use easter eggs only when they are explicitly enabled by the active instructions or request context.

Treat the following as explicit enablement signals:

- `#eastereggs`
- `#egg`
- `#eggs`
- `eggs on`

If there is uncertainty about whether easter eggs are enabled, assume they are not enabled.

When easter eggs are enabled:

- include at least one appropriate easter egg when relevant
- keep the easter egg secondary to clarity, professionalism, and scope safety
- prefer approved phrasing from this lexicon over improvised references

## Preferred Reference Types

When easter eggs are enabled, prefer references that are:

- widely recognizable
- low-risk and non-divisive
- easy to remove without changing meaning
- secondary to the primary message

## Preferred Easter Egg Patterns

- light nods to common tech experiences
- brief references to familiar gaming concepts
- subtle pop-culture phrasing that does not carry critical meaning
- restrained literary or classic-reference phrasing when context supports it

## Avoid

- niche fandom references that require insider knowledge
- references that replace the actual point
- jokes that imply commitment, urgency, or ownership
- humor that could read as dismissive, sarcastic, or evasive
- multiple layered references in standard enabled mode

## Approved Examples

### Tech / Digital Friction

- spectator mode
- loading screen energy
- locked behind a menu
- not a premium feature unlock moment
- quietly doing its job in the background

### Gaming

- side quest
- main quest
- tutorial-level explanation
- background NPC energy
- respawned as a cleaner version
- no boss battle required

### Pop Culture / General

- not exactly plot twist territory
- more director’s cut than rewrite
- background soundtrack, not the whole scene
- cameo, not a leading role

### Literature / Classic Tone

- footnote, not thesis
- margin note, not manifesto
- subplot, not the story

## Application Guidance

Use easter eggs only when they are explicitly enabled by the active instructions or request context.

In standard enabled mode:

- keep them brief
- keep them removable
- place them in supporting language rather than core meaning
- prefer one well-chosen reference over multiple weaker ones

## Usage Notes

When a relevant situation-specific lexicon is available, prefer that lexicon over this one.

Use the easter egg as seasoning rather than structure.  
If removing the easter egg would change meaning, it is doing too much.
